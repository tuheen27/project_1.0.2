The Science of Aurora

The aurora borealis, or northern lights, occurs when charged particles from the sun collide with gases in Earth's atmosphere. These particles are directed toward the poles by Earth's magnetic field, creating the dancing lights we observe.

Did you know?

The different colors of the aurora are caused by different gases. Oxygen produces green and red light, while nitrogen creates blue and purple hues.
